Case 01 · Content Platform

Predictive Risk Analytics & Content Moderation

Designed a 3-stage framework to shift from reactive to preventive risk management at scale

Risk ML Ops Analytics
Challenge
A content platform relied on spreadsheets and post-publication review to detect violations — reactive, slow, and costly to operate at scale.
Approach
  • Centralized real-time data source replacing ad-hoc sheets
  • Unified risk score from behavioral signals & policy rules
  • Trained prediction model in shadow mode (no flow disruption)
  • Phased rollout: risk-based moderation → model retraining loop
  • Defined escalation thresholds per risk category
Results & Metrics
Pre-publication prevention rate — violations blocked before reaching users
Cost per moderated asset — infrastructure + reviewer time + overhead
Post-publication violation rate — quality signal for model accuracy
Case 02 · Expert Marketplace

Abuse & Zero Tolerance Policy Design

Built an automated enforcement cycle connecting NLP moderation, QC control, and a sanctions system

Policy NLP Trust & Safety
Challenge
No formalized policy for prohibited interactions between users and service providers, leading to uncontrolled refunds, reputational risk, and inconsistent enforcement.
Approach
  • Defined behavioral markers on both user and provider sides
  • NLP model flags prohibited content pre-send, blocks message
  • Risk engine logs events and applies progressive sanctions
  • High-risk users flagged for QC human review
  • Policy presented at onboarding + contextual reminders
Business Impact
Targeted high-confidence violations only → minimal false positive revenue loss
KPI: Refunds linked to prohibited content ↓, cost per moderated asset ↓
Justified to leadership: safety policy lowers existing revenue leakage

Case 04 · Data Services Company

Data Annotation Project Launch at Scale

Launched two parallel annotation teams from zero to full capacity, managing high turnover and quality targets

Project Management Hiring Quality
Challenge
Scale two annotation teams (3D LiDAR + 2D image) to 30 annotators each over 6 weeks with 50% and 20% respective turnover rates. Deliver 100k+ LiDAR frames and 200k+ 2D images to quality standards.
Approach
  • Waterfall framework: Setup → Ramp-up → Steady-state → Wrap-up
  • Agile execution: weekly throughput targets, daily 15-min team syncs
  • Hiring pipeline ahead of need by 2 weeks (buffer for churn)
  • Week 1 training-only track: guidelines + benchmark, no production
  • Bonus system tied to quality score, not speed alone
  • Weekly client status updates via KAM
Success Metrics
≥95% quality score after first QA pass
≤2 weeks time-to-productivity for new hires
Weekly throughput ≥ plan from Week 6, client NPS "green"
Case 05 · Operations Consulting

RPA Automation: From 80% Ops to Strategic Management

Built a prioritization framework and rollout roadmap to automate 40% of team working time

RPA Change Management Strategy
Challenge
Operations managers spent ~80% of time on manual routine tasks, leaving only 20% for strategic work. Goal: flip the ratio using automation, with no headcount reduction as the primary outcome.
Approach
  • Process selection criteria: rule-based, high-frequency, structured data, stable, <15% exceptions
  • Impact × Effort matrix for prioritization (hours × risk × headcount vs. dev effort)
  • 2-week sprints: one automation per sprint, demo after each
  • Freed time redirected: strategy → client experience → cross-training
  • Each manager builds a new time allocation with their lead; 30-day review
Results
Target: 40% of team time automated within 6 months
ROI: ≥3x per bot within 12 months
Strategic time share: 20% → 40%+, bot uptime ≥99%

Operating Principles

I build systems that outlast my involvement. Every engagement starts with clarity on what's being measured, and ends with a team that can operate without me.
1
Data before decisions
Every process change is grounded in measurement. I define success metrics before implementation, not after.
2
Waterfall structure, Agile delivery
I use long-range planning for accountability and short cycles for adaptation. Both matter.
3
Automate the routine, elevate the human
Automation isn't about reducing headcount — it's about redirecting talent to work that requires judgment.
4
Escalation paths, not heroics
Good operations mean problems surface early and travel to the right person — not that one person firefights everything.
